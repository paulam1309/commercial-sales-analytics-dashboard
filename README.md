## Overview

This repository contains a business sales analysis project developed in a commercial context, focused on supporting managerial decision-making through data visualization and KPI analysis.

The objective was to build a dashboard that allows the commercial management team to:

- Monitor sales performance
- Detect risks and opportunities
- Support fast decision-making in weekly meetings

---

## Business Context

A consumer goods company operates across multiple:

- Regions
- Sales channels
- Products

The commercial team required a dashboard to evaluate sales performance using structured transactional data.

---

## Dataset

The dataset includes:

- Date
- Region
- Channel
- Product
- Units Sold
- Sales Revenue ($)

---

## Key Business Questions

This analysis helps answer:

- Which regions generate the highest revenue?
- Which sales channels perform better?
- What products drive the majority of sales?
- Are there underperforming segments?
- How do sales evolve over time?

---

## Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

- Total Revenue
- Total Units Sold
- Revenue by Region
- Revenue by Channel
- Revenue by Product
- Sales Trend Over Time

These KPIs enable high-level monitoring and operational analysis.

---

## Dashboard Components

The dashboard includes:

- Revenue trend over time
- Sales by region (comparative view)
- Sales by channel
- Product performance breakdown
- Interactive filter (Region / Channel / Date)

Design principles applied:

- Clear visual hierarchy
- Clean labeling
- Business-oriented layout
- Fast interpretability

---

## Key Insights

Example insights derived from the analysis:

1. Certain regions concentrate the majority of revenue, indicating potential dependency risk.
2. One sales channel outperforms others consistently, suggesting strategic investment focus.

---

## Business Recommendations

Based on the analysis:

- Strengthen high-performing channels
- Investigate underperforming regions
- Optimize product mix in low-revenue segments
- Implement periodic KPI monitoring for weekly commercial meetings
